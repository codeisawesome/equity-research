3. Fundamental Analysis

Full Analysis

IREN is in the middle of a radical business model overhaul. Historically relying on the highly cyclical, capital-intensive mining of Bitcoin, which provided daily liquid rewards but zero predictability, the company is pivoting toward an AI Cloud Service Provider model. This shifts revenue to longer-duration, amortized contracts and annualized run-rate revenue with hyperscale counterparties. SEC

Trailing 12-month revenue growth sits at 163%, but historical CAGRs are less useful as the revenue mix transitions. Profitability trends currently look chaotic; trailing 12-month net profit margin is 46.4%, but trailing operating margin is -19.1%. This divergence is driven by large non-cash accounting adjustments rather than clean operational cash generation. Looking forward, management has described the Microsoft project as carrying an estimated ~85% project EBITDA margin, but that remains a management estimate rather than a realized reported margin. Finnhub Earnings Call

IREN’s competitive moat is exceptional and expanding. In the AI infrastructure space, GPUs are commoditized, but gigawatt-scale, grid-connected power is extremely scarce. By securing 4.5 GW of power across ERCOT, British Columbia, and newly acquired SPP territory in Oklahoma, IREN possesses large-scale capacity advantages. Their ability to deploy air-cooled GPUs into legacy mining facilities addresses a hyperscaler pain point: time-to-market. SEC

Management’s track record is a key part of the story, but the funding language has to be narrower than the original report suggested. Management has secured financing that management says covers roughly 95% of the GPU-related capex for Horizons 1-4 tied to the Microsoft contract when combined with Microsoft prepayments, but this should not be generalized to “the buildout” as a whole. IREN has also raised substantial equity recently, including a December 2025 registered direct offering and prior ATM issuance. Insider alignment is still meaningful, but the “rather than dilutive equity” framing is not supportable. SEC

4. Balance Sheet Deep Dive

Full Analysis

Asset Quality: As of December 31, 2025, total assets were $7.03B, total liabilities were $4.52B, stockholders’ equity was $2.51B, and cash and cash equivalents were $3.26B. Current assets were $3.38B against current liabilities of $682.1M, implying a current ratio of about 4.96x. Goodwill was not reported; intangible assets were $107.6M, so “negligible intangibles” has to be softened into a more precise statement. SEC Calc

Debt Structure: The balance sheet already carried $3.685B of convertible notes payable at December 31, 2025. The additional roughly $3.6B GS/JPM GPU financing was disclosed as a commitment subject to definitive documents and conditions, not as fully closed funded debt. SEC

Covenant and Credit Risk: Because the disclosed GPU financing is tied to a Microsoft project and backed by a $1.9B customer prepayment, classic covenant pressure is less severe than it would be for unsecured speculative capex. But that is not the same thing as saying the balance sheet is functionally anchored by Microsoft alone; the company still carries real leverage, conversion complexity, and dilution optionality. SEC

Off-Balance-Sheet Exposure: The primary exposure remains the scale of GPU procurement and data-center build commitments. Those commitments are partly supported by prepayments and financing commitments, but they are still large enough to make broader capital access relevant. SEC

Liquidity Position: Liquidity is materially stronger than the stale June 30, 2025 figures implied. Cash was $3.26B at the latest filing date and current ratio was about 4.96x. That said, the proper takeaway is strong liquidity, not a blanket claim that all funding risk has disappeared. SEC Calc

Net-Net Assessment: The balance sheet is still a source of strength because of the large cash balance and power-asset buildout, but it is more levered, more complex, and more dilution-sensitive than the original report claimed. SEC
