3. Fundamental Analysis

Full Analysis

Amprius generates revenue by engineering and selling ultra-high energy density silicon anode batteries. Historically reliant on its premium SiMaxx (silicon nanowire) product, the company has successfully pivoted its volume strategy to SiCore, a highly manufacturable cell that is driving current margin expansion. The geographic and customer mix is highly concentrated in the U.S. unmanned aerial systems (UAS) and defense sectors, meaning revenue is largely driven by sticky, recurring purchase orders rather than one-time consumer sales.

Historically, growth was negligible as the company operated as an R&D lab. However, the inflection is stark: revenue surged 202% YoY to $73M in FY2025, up from $8.8M in 2023. Forward estimates anchor on management's $125M 2026 guide, representing a 70% forward CAGR. SEC Earnings Call

Profitability trends are the strongest pillar of the fundamental thesis. Gross margins have violently reversed from -166.9% in FY2023 to a record +24% in Q4 2025. Operating margins remain negative on a trailing basis but are rapidly approaching breakeven as OpEx stays flat while revenue scales. ROE and ROA remain deeply negative (-38% and -23% TTM, respectively) but are lagging indicators of the prior capital-intensive structure. SEC

Amprius's competitive moat is rooted in proprietary performance and regulatory positioning. The silicon anode technology delivers up to 450 Wh/kg, offering an unmatched weight-to-power ratio critical for aviation. More importantly, their NDAA-compliant supply chain acts as a massive regulatory moat, effectively locking out cheaper Chinese competitors (like DJI-affiliated suppliers) from lucrative Department of Defense (DoD) and Defense Innovation Unit (DIU) contracts. Earnings Call

Management scores highly on strategic execution but poorly on shareholder alignment. President Tom Stepien and CFO Ricardo Rodriguez flawlessly executed the pivot to contract manufacturing and protected margins. However, aggressive ATM equity dilution and recent massive insider sales by the CTO warrant close monitoring. SEC

4. Balance Sheet Deep Dive

Full Analysis

Asset quality: The asset base is clean and increasingly liquid. Total assets sit at $121.1M as of Q4 2024, up from $107.7M in 2023. The company carries zero reported goodwill or complex intangibles, meaning equity is tangible. No historical goodwill impairments have been recorded. SEC

Debt structure: AMPX operates with zero long-term debt. The balance sheet is entirely unlevered, meaning there is no weighted average cost of debt, no floating rate exposure, and no debt maturity schedule to navigate in the next 1–5 years. SEC

Covenant and credit risk: Unrated by major agencies due to the lack of debt. Without debt, there are no restrictive financial covenants or Debt/EBITDA thresholds to breach, virtually eliminating traditional credit risk.

Off-balance-sheet exposure: The most significant off-balance-sheet risk was eliminated in early 2026. Management paid a $20M termination fee (recognized as a one-time impairment) to exit a massive operating lease obligation for a Colorado manufacturing facility. This bold move wiped out >$110M in future CapEx and lease liabilities. Earnings Call

Liquidity position: Cash equivalents sit at $55.2M (though Q3 2025 indicated ~$73M prior to the lease termination fee). The current ratio is an exceptionally robust 4.00, and the quick ratio is 3.61. Given that adjusted EBITDA turned positive in Q4 2025, the company has effectively halted its operational cash burn, giving it indefinite runway barring a collapse in top-line revenue. SEC

Net-net assessment: The balance sheet is a definitive source of strength, acting as a fortress of unlevered liquidity that supports their asset-light growth phase.

5. Cash Flow Analysis

Full Analysis

Operating Cash Flow (OCF) and Free Cash Flow (FCF) have historically been the weakest links for Amprius. In 2023 and 2024, the company burned -$43.1M and -$36.6M in FCF, respectively. However, Q4 2025 marked a paradigm shift with the printing of +$1.8M in adjusted EBITDA. While GAAP OCF may still show slight lumpiness due to working capital builds for the $53M backlog, the trajectory is firmly pointing toward sustained FCF generation by late 2026. SEC

CapEx as a percentage of revenue plummeted from a devastating 199.6% in 2023 to 13.6% in 2024, and will fall further into the single digits in 2026 due to the termination of the Colorado facility and reliance on contract manufacturing. Calc

Cash flow quality is currently clouded by the massive one-time impairment charge for the lease termination, which severely distorts GAAP net income vs. OCF. However, working capital management (DIO, DSO) is stabilizing as the company transitions to fulfilling large, recurring DoD/defense purchase orders rather than erratic pilot programs. SEC

Capital Allocation Scorecard:

  • Buybacks: Zero. The company is in rapid growth mode; shares outstanding have aggressively expanded, not shrunk.
  • Dividends: Zero. Standard for an early-stage hardware tech company.
  • Debt management: Excellent. They have maintained a zero-debt profile, funding the "valley of death" entirely through equity.
  • M&A track record: N/A. Amprius is growing organically through internal R&D and strategic contract manufacturing partnerships (e.g., Nanotech Energy), avoiding goodwill-bloated acquisitions.

Sustainability verdict: Amprius is on the verge of being able to self-fund its highly reduced CapEx requirements from operating cash flow, successfully pivoting away from a reliance on the At-The-Market (ATM) equity facility.

In Q4 2025, the company reported an adjusted EPS of -$0.01 against a GAAP net loss that was dragged down by a $22.5M one-time impairment. This spread is legitimate, as the impairment represents a non-recurring strategic exit from a real estate lease, but it heavily distorts bottom-line optics. SEC

"One-time" charge frequency: Generally clean. The recent $22.5M charge is the only major restructuring/impairment event in the trailing 12 quarters. It is a true one-time strategic pivot rather than "recurring-in-disguise." SEC

Revenue recognition signals: Clean. Revenue is recognized upon delivery/fulfillment of purchase orders. Growth is backed by a verifiable $53.3M order backlog. There are no signs of aggressive pull-forward tactics; in fact, management has historically under-promised on the pipeline. SEC

Accrual quality: Given the historical unprofitability, the Sloan Accrual Ratio is highly volatile. However, OCF has generally mirrored adjusted operating losses. There are no glaring signs of channel stuffing or aggressive accrual capitalization.

Audit and disclosure flags: Clean. No auditor changes, going-concern language, or material weaknesses in SOX 404 controls reported in the most recent 10-K. SEC

While management is touting extreme confidence on earnings calls, massive selling by core technologists creates a severe "talk vs. action" mismatch. SEC

Overall accounting confidence: Minor Concerns. The core financial reporting is highly transparent and the lease write-off is fundamentally sound, but the heavy insider selling combined with heavy ATM dilution over the last 18 months warrants caution for swing traders.
