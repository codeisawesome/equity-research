Bloom Energy generates revenue by manufacturing, installing, and servicing SOFC platforms (Bloom Energy Servers) that convert natural gas or hydrogen into electricity without combustion. Revenue is split between product sales (hardware) and recurring service contracts. Historically, the company ground out a 15–25% top-line CAGR, constrained by high upfront costs and geographic reliance on states with high grid power costs. That trajectory has snapped upward; trailing FY25 revenue hit $2.0B (+38.9% YoY), with forward 2026 estimates implying >50% growth. Profitability is undergoing a radical transition. Gross margins languished between 13–22% for years but expanded to 29.4% in FY25. More importantly, the historically bleeding service segment finally achieved a 20% non-GAAP gross margin in Q4. Operating margins flipped from a dismal -23.0% in 2022 to +3.6% in 2025. ROE and ROA remain negative on a trailing basis due to heavy GAAP stock-based compensation and accumulated deficits, but the cash generation inflection is real. Bloom's competitive moat is currently immense. It blends a scale advantage (few competitors can mass-produce megawatt-scale fuel cells) with a pricing power/switching cost dynamic driven by its native 800V DC output. Management explicitly noted in the MD&A and earnings calls that adapting traditional AC combustion turbines for AI racks requires 'Band-Aids' (rectifiers and transformers) that ruin efficiency. Management quality, led by founder KR Sridhar, has proven resilient. Beating an aggressive 90-day Oracle delivery timeline by 35 days showcased elite operational control. However, capital allocation remains heavily tilted toward dilution (R&D and SBC) rather than shareholder returns, and the abrupt departure of the CFO in Q1 2025 remains a mildly unresolved footnote.

Bloom's cash flow profile recently crossed the rubicon from cash-incinerator to self-sustaining. Free Cash Flow (FCF) followed suit, posting $33.1M and $57.2M, representing a 2.9% FCF margin in 2025. However, cash flow quality requires vigilance. The cash conversion cycle is strained by the need to stockpile inventory ($643M in FY25) to meet hyperscaler timelines. A growing gap between non-GAAP net income and OCF could signal that revenue is being recognized faster than cash is collected. Capital allocation scorecard: Buybacks: None.
